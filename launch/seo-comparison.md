## Article: "EchoType vs MonkeyType vs Keybr: Which Typing Tool Actually Makes You Better?"

---

### Introduction

There are dozens of typing tools online, but they fall into two categories: **speed tests** (measure how fast you are) and **training tools** (help you improve). Most people use speed tests and wonder why they plateau.

This comparison looks at the three most interesting approaches:
- **MonkeyType** — The gold standard speed test
- **Keybr** — Algorithm-driven key training with fake words
- **EchoType** — Adaptive real-sentence practice targeting your personal weaknesses

### Quick Comparison

| Feature | MonkeyType | Keybr | EchoType |
|---------|-----------|-------|----------|
| Purpose | Speed measurement | Key isolation | Adaptive training |
| Text type | Random words / quotes | Pseudo-words (fake) | Real sentences (adaptive) |
| Personalization | Visual themes only | Per-key difficulty | Full error fingerprint |
| Tracks bigrams | No | Partially | Yes (with timing) |
| Difficulty adapts | No | Yes (adds new letters) | Yes (selects weak sentences) |
| Custom text | Yes (paste mode) | No | Yes |
| Progress metric | WPM only | Per-key confidence | Flow Score (speed + accuracy + consistency) |
| Account required | Optional | Optional | No (localStorage) |
| Price | Free | Free | Free |
| Best for | Benchmarking your speed | Fixing one weak key | Sustained improvement on real text |

### When to Use Each

**Use MonkeyType when:** You want to know your current WPM, compete on leaderboards, or just enjoy the zen of typing random words. It's the best speed test available.

**Use Keybr when:** You have a specific key that's holding you back (like a weak pinky on 'p' or slow number row). Keybr will drill that one key mercilessly until it's fixed. It's surgical.

**Use EchoType when:** You've plateaued on MonkeyType and want to actually improve. You want to practice on real text (not fake words), with the content automatically selected to challenge your specific weak patterns. You want to see your "typing DNA" and track which weaknesses are improving over time.

### The Key Difference

MonkeyType is a **thermometer** — it tells you your temperature.
Keybr is a **scalpel** — it fixes one specific problem.
EchoType is a **coach** — it diagnoses all your weaknesses and builds a training plan from real text.

### Conclusion

These tools aren't competitors — they're complementary:
1. **Start with MonkeyType** to benchmark your baseline
2. **Use EchoType daily** for adaptive practice on real sentences
3. **Use Keybr** for surgical fixes on one specific problem key
4. **Re-test on MonkeyType** to see your improvement

Try EchoType free at echotype.app — no account needed, your data stays in your browser.
